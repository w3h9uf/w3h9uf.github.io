Tide

Effective 2026-06-11

Privacy Policy

Tide is built to help you pause before opening distracting apps while keeping your personal patterns private.

The short version

  • Tide does not require an account.
  • Tide does not run a server that receives your app selections, check-ins, or usage patterns.
  • Tide does not include advertising or third-party analytics SDKs.
  • Your Screen Time selections and Tide check-ins stay on your device.
  • Subscription purchases are handled by Apple through the App Store.

Data Tide uses on your device

DataPurpose
Selected apps or categoriesUsed by Apple's Screen Time APIs to know which apps should show a Tide pause.
Emotional check-insUsed to personalize your reflection history and feeling map.
Pause outcomesUsed to show streaks, skipped opens, and local progress.
SettingsUsed to remember your pause duration, morning card time, and subscription state.

Screen Time and Family Controls

Tide uses Apple's Family Controls, Managed Settings, and Device Activity frameworks. These frameworks allow you to choose apps to guard and allow Tide to show a pause experience when needed. Tide does not upload your app selections to Hashing Corp.

Data storage

Tide stores app settings, check-ins, and local summary data in the app's sandbox on your device. This data is removed when you delete the app, except for any backups managed by Apple through iCloud or encrypted device backups.

Subscriptions

Tide Pro is sold through Apple's App Store. Apple processes payment and manages subscription status. Tide receives only the entitlement state needed to unlock Pro features.

What we do not collect

  • No name, email, phone number, or account identifier is required to use Tide.
  • No advertising identifier is collected.
  • No precise location data is collected.
  • No contact list, photos, messages, or browsing content is collected.

Children

Tide is intended for people who manage their own device habits. It is not directed to children under 13.

Changes

If this policy changes, we will update the effective date above and describe material changes in release notes when appropriate.
